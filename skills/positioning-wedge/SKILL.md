# Positioning Wedge

Turn market research into a **defensible wedge** buyers understand. Requires competitor context from `market-research` or `competitor-landscape`.

## April Dunford checklist

Answer in order:

1. **Competitive alternatives** — what would customers do if you did not exist? (Include non-SaaS workarounds.)
2. **Unique attributes** — what do you have that alternatives lack?
3. **Value** — what benefit do those attributes enable?
4. **Target customers** — who cares most (subset of ICP)?
5. **Market category** — what frame makes your value obvious?
6. **Trend** — why now? (Link to market memo **Why now**.)

## Positioning canvas

| Field       | Content              |
| ----------- | -------------------- |
| For         | Target segment       |
| Who         | Struggle / job       |
| Our product | Category name        |
| Provides    | Key value            |
| Unlike      | Primary alternative  |
| We          | Differentiated proof |

## Wedge statement

One sentence: **[Segment]** struggling with **[job]** choose us over **[alternative]** because **[proof]** — unlike **[incumbent weakness]**.

## Messaging implications

- Headline angle (not final copy)
- 3 proof points grounded in evidence URLs or quotes
- What **not** to claim (unsupported)

## Kill / pivot cues

- Wedge requires educating a category that does not exist and buyers do not search
- Same promise as incumbent with no proof of 10× on one dimension that matters
